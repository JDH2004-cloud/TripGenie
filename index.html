Sample itinerary

Lisbon on a student-friendly budget

Day 1: Alfama, viewpoints, and cheap eats

Start with a tram ride into Alfama, walk between miradouros, and keep lunch simple with bifanas or a market stall meal. Save paid attractions for one or two must-see stops.

Day 2: Belem and riverside wandering

Use public transit to reach Belem early, share pastries with friends, then spend the afternoon along the waterfront before a low-cost dinner in Bairro Alto.

Day 3: Sintra or beach day

Pick one day trip instead of trying to do everything. Sintra works for castles and views; Cascais works when you want a lower-effort beach reset.

Packing list

Carry-on essentials

  • Comfortable walking shoes and one nicer evening layer
  • Portable charger, universal adapter, and offline maps
  • Reusable bottle, compact rain shell, and day bag
  • Copies of passport, booking confirmations, and insurance
Budget tips

Spend where it matters

  • Book central lodging when it cuts down on rideshares.
  • Use lunch specials for restaurants and keep dinners casual.
  • Group nearby attractions to avoid extra transit costs.
  • Reserve one paid experience, then fill the rest with free walks.
